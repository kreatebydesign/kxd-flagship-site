# KHIG — Product Emotion Map

**Edition 1 · Phase 16C**

Every major workspace has one dominant emotional objective. Design, copy, and hierarchy should serve that objective — nothing else competes.

---

## Workspace → Emotion

| Workspace | Emotion | Objective |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| **Intelligence** | Confidence | Leave knowing what matters and why |
| **Timeline** | Progress | See the partnership arc — how far you've come |
| **Work Engine** | Momentum | Feel forward motion on what needs doing today |
| **Clients / Accounts** | Relationships | Understand the health of each partnership |
| **Review Workspace** | Resolution | Close the loop on client requests with clarity |
| **Review Inbox** | Resolution | Triage with intention, not urgency theater |
| **Portal** | Partnership | Clients feel cared for, informed, and included |
| **Operations / Command** | Control | See the whole studio without drowning in detail |
| **Executive Dashboard** | Clarity | One calm view of business health |

---

## Ritual Modes → Emotion

| Ritual | Emotion | When |
|--------|---------|------|
| **Morning Brief** | Confidence | First open of the day |
| **Focus Mode** | Momentum | Ready to execute |
| **Weekly Review** | Progress | End of week reflection |
| **Planning** *(future)* | Clarity | Week ahead orientation |

---

## Design Implications

1. **One dominant feeling per screen** — if a second emotion competes, remove or demote it
2. **Subtraction over addition** — calm interfaces beat comprehensive ones
3. **Executive language** — observations, not alerts; guidance, not dashboards
4. **Delight is quiet** — affirmations, not gamification
5. **Reading rhythm** — narratives at 44rem; sections breathe with token spacing

---

## Audit Checklist

For each screen, ask:

- What is the ONE emotion this screen should create?
- Does the first thing the eye sees serve that emotion?
- Can anything disappear?
- Can anything become quieter?
- Does the copy sound like a confident executive briefing?
