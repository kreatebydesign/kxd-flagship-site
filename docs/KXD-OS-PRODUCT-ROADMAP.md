# KXD OS Product Roadmap

**Version 1.0 · Long-Term Product Compass**  
**Status:** Living document — product strategy, not engineering schedule  
**Horizon:** 5–10 years  
**Audience:** Founder, product, engineering, Cursor agents

> This is **not** an engineering roadmap. Engineering phases live in the platform registry.  
> This document defines **where KXD OS is going** and **why** — connecting experience, intelligence, and business evolution into one strategy.

---

## Document purpose

KXD OS is being built to progressively run more of Kreate by Design while the founder focuses on leadership, relationships, strategy, investment, and growth.

This roadmap connects the governing documents:

| Document | Role |
|----------|------|
| `KXD-OS-CONSTITUTION.md` | Experience standard — how it must feel |
| `KXD-OS-VISUAL-MANIFESTO.md` | Visual craft — how it must look |
| `STUDIO-INTELLIGENCE-ARCHITECTURE.md` | Intelligence layer — how capability composes |
| Platform architecture / registry | Implementation truth — what exists and what ships |

**Product compass governs priority.**  
**Constitution governs experience.**  
**Studio Intelligence Architecture governs intelligence.**  
**Platform registry governs build order.**

When prioritizing features, ask: *Which era does this advance? Does it strengthen the flywheel? Does it deepen the moat?*

---

## North Star (unchanged)

KXD OS evolves from software that **organizes** work → **understands** work → **prepares** work → **executes** repeatable work — becoming **calmer as it becomes more capable**.

The ultimate KPI is **founder time reclaimed**, not features shipped.

---

## Era overview

Six eras describe product maturity — not release trains. Eras overlap. Kreate by Design may live in multiple eras across different pillars simultaneously.

```
Foundation ──► Memory ──► Guidance ──► Automation ──► Autonomous Studio ──► Founder Freedom
   │              │           │              │                  │                    │
   NOW         ~1–2 yr      ~2–4 yr        ~4–6 yr            ~6–8 yr              ~8–10 yr
```

| Era | One line |
|-----|----------|
| **Foundation** | The studio has one operating environment |
| **Memory** | The studio remembers every relationship and decision |
| **Guidance** | The OS prepares and advises — founder commits |
| **Automation** | Repeatable studio rituals run themselves |
| **Autonomous Studio** | Delivery operates; founder handles exceptions |
| **Founder Freedom** | The OS runs the studio; the founder leads the company |

## Era 1 — Foundation

**Approximate horizon:** Now through early maturity of core modules

### Mission

Establish KXD OS as the **single private operating environment** for Kreate by Design — replacing scattered tools, admin panels, and tribal knowledge with one studio-grade system.

### Primary focus

- Unified operations shell and navigation
- Client Command as the relationship home
- Work Items as the execution layer
- Activity Engine and timeline as emerging memory
- Portal as the client-facing surface
- Business Development, Creative, and Finance modules connected to shared core
- Constitution, Visual Manifesto, and architectural discipline

### Major capabilities introduced

- Operations cockpit (`Today`, `Work`, `Clients`)
- Client Command workspace (tabs, timeline, actions, communications, financial)
- Work Items with portfolio and per-client boards
- Portal (requests, deliverables, reports, projects)
- Sales pipeline (leads, proposals, conversion)
- Creative Engine (campaigns, flyers, video, social)
- Playbooks (definition and manual launch)
- Platform registry and edition framework
- Executive reporting and website audits

### Studio Intelligence maturity

**Level 0 → Level 1 (Advises)** — emerging

- Deterministic Client Memory (signals, scores, next best actions)
- Rule-based recommendations on Intelligence tab
- Work cockpit briefs and focus lists
- No chatbot; no sidebar intelligence product
- Intelligence is sparse and evidence-backed

### Founder experience

Opens one environment each morning. Still navigates modules. Still spawns much of the work manually. Feels pride in the interface — calm, premium, intentional — but still **operates** the studio actively.

### Employee experience

Uses Payload admin and operations modules. Learning is mostly tribal — documentation and founder oversight. Junior creators have defined workflows but limited in-context teaching.

### Client experience

Portal feels like a private client HQ — not a generic dashboard. Submits requests, views deliverables and reports. Relationship with KXD feels held and professional.

### Business impact

Operational fragmentation ends. One source of truth for clients, work, and revenue. Founder stops context-switching between five tools.

### Success metric

**Single-environment adoption** — 90%+ of studio operations run inside KXD OS, not parallel spreadsheets or external PM tools.

## Era 2 — Memory

**Approximate horizon:** Years 1–2 from Foundation completion

### Mission

Make KXD OS the **institutional memory** of every client relationship — nothing important lives only in someone's head or inbox.

### Primary focus

- Timeline unification — Activity Engine as sole relationship memory
- Client Command consolidation as canonical workspace
- Complete relationship history (communications, decisions, outcomes)
- Knowledge capture from work — corrections, approvals, launches
- Portfolio-level relationship visibility

### Major capabilities introduced

- Unified executive timeline (portfolio + per-client)
- Complete activity backfill and dedupe discipline
- Communications and meeting memory integrated into Client Command
- Client Success foundation (Attention Needed, Recommendations, Wins)
- Executive Notes and Strategy linked to clients and decisions
- Brain layer — portfolio synthesis across clients (read-only)
- Knowledge vault — studio standards and SOPs (founder-authored)

### Studio Intelligence maturity

**Level 1 (Advises)** — mature

- Explain Why on every recommendation
- Client-scoped memory snapshots with citations
- Portfolio health synthesis for Founder / Today
- Suppression rules from dismissals (3× pattern)
- Intelligence tab remains explicit; Overview teasers grow richer

### Founder experience

Opens a client and **knows the story immediately** — last contact, open commitments, recent wins, what needs attention. Stops reconstructing context before calls. Trusts the timeline.

### Employee experience

New team members read the relationship history instead of asking the founder. Handoffs between people don't lose context.

### Client experience

Feels remembered. Follow-ups reference prior work accurately. Portal and communications reflect relationship continuity.

### Business impact

Relationship quality improves. Mistakes from lost context decline. Founder prep time before client interactions drops sharply.

### Success metric

**Context reconstruction time** — founder spends &lt;5 minutes preparing for any client interaction because the OS already holds the story.

## Era 3 — Guidance

**Approximate horizon:** Years 2–4

### Mission

Studio Intelligence **prepares work and guides decisions** — the founder commits, but rarely starts from blank.

### Primary focus

- Assistance patterns embedded in workflow (Prepare This, Review My Work, Draft Reply)
- Client Success maturity (sparse Growth Opportunities, evidence-required)
- Teaching in context for employees
- Standards checks before ship (creative, proposals, launches)
- Learning from corrections and approvals

### Major capabilities introduced

- Prepared drafts (communications, proposal sections, work item descriptions)
- Standards linting against KXD and client canon
- In-context teaching (Show Me How) for roles and workflows
- Growth Opportunities with mandatory "why now?"
- Reply ready from communication threads
- Meeting follow-up preparation
- Promotion flow: correction → studio standard candidate
- Client voice memory from edit history

### Studio Intelligence maturity

**Level 1–2 (Advises → Prepares)** — primary era

- Prepare This is default for recurring doc types
- Recommend Next Step drives cockpit focus
- Intelligence becomes **less visible as a tab**, more visible **in the moment**
- Every prepared output cites evidence
- Still no auto-send, no auto-pricing, no chatbot

### Founder experience

Rarely starts from zero. Reviews and commits prepared work. Spends time on judgment, relationships, and creative direction — not assembly. Sunday evenings feel calm.

### Employee experience

Guided through unfamiliar workflows. Standards checks catch mistakes before founder review. Quality becomes more consistent with less oversight.

### Client experience

Faster, more consistent delivery. Communications feel considered. Fewer dropped balls.

### Business impact

Throughput increases without headcount. Proposal and delivery quality standardizes. Founder hours shift from production to leadership.

### Success metric

**Prepared work acceptance rate** — &gt;70% of prepared drafts accepted with minor edits or less; repeat correction rate declining quarter over quarter.

## Era 4 — Automation

**Approximate horizon:** Years 4–6

### Mission

**Repeatable studio rituals execute themselves** — playbooks absorb what the team does the same way every time.

### Primary focus

- Playbook maturity — define, approve, run, monitor
- Event-driven work spawning (requests, audits, launches, renewals)
- Level 3 execution with approval gates
- Automation publishers across Creative, Deliverables, Portal, Infrastructure
- Suggest Automation and Create Playbook from recurring patterns

### Major capabilities introduced

- Approved playbooks run on schedule and on event
- Work items spawn automatically from portal, audits, and health monitors
- Launch sequences, onboarding, and monthly delivery as playbook chains
- Attention Needed items auto-created from monitors (SSL, stale contact, audit score)
- Single-approval execution for safe internal actions
- Playbook run visibility in Today and Founder views
- Lead funnel unification — BD automation handoffs to Genesis

### Studio Intelligence maturity

**Level 2–3 (Prepares → Executes with approval)**

- Fix For Me on trusted internal content
- Playbook step preparation and queue-for-approval
- Suggest Automation surfaces after stable pattern detection
- Level 4 begins for **named, audited, low-risk playbooks only**

### Founder experience

Approves batches, not steps. Reviews exceptions and blockers. Monthly rituals happen without being remembered. Founder calendar has fewer operational meetings.

### Employee experience

Runs playbooks with teaching annotations. Escalates blockers. Less manual status updating.

### Client experience

Onboarding feels seamless. Health issues caught before they notice. Deliverables arrive on rhythm.

### Business impact

Capacity grows without linear hiring. Margins improve. Delivery consistency becomes a competitive advantage.

### Success metric

**Playbook coverage** — &gt;60% of recurring studio rituals run via approved playbooks; founder manual spawns decline &gt;50% from Guidance era baseline.

## Era 5 — Autonomous Studio

**Approximate horizon:** Years 6–8

### Mission

KXD OS **operates the studio's delivery engine** — the founder leads; the OS runs.

### Primary focus

- Level 4 playbook execution for approved domains
- Exception-only founder attention
- Cross-pillar orchestration (BD win → Genesis → Command → Work → Portal)
- Portfolio autonomy — health monitors, renewals, reporting cycles
- Calmer UI as capability grows — fewer dashboards, more briefs

### Major capabilities introduced

- Unattended playbook runs for health checks, reporting, renewals, deliverable reminders
- End-to-end client lifecycle orchestration
- Auto-resolution of known patterns (expired snoozes, routine status transitions)
- Portfolio Client Success with sparse, high-confidence signals only
- Founder chair — strategic view, not operational inventory
- Live analytics feeding Wins and Recommendations automatically

### Studio Intelligence maturity

**Level 3–4 (Executes with approval → Runs approved playbooks)**

- Intelligence is **invisible** — surfaced only at decision moments
- More intelligence, less interface
- Trust model fully enforced — money, legal, client send remain human forever
- Brain provides portfolio narrative for quarterly review, not daily noise

### Founder experience

Three meetings and one approval session can run a month. Opens OS for relationships, strategy, and exceptions — not to check if work happened. Trusts the studio is running.

### Employee experience

Works inside guided contexts. Studio teaches standards continuously. Founder reviews edge cases, not routine output.

### Client experience

KXD feels like a premium partner with institutional memory and flawless follow-through. Portal is always current. Proactive recommendations feel earned, not salesy.

### Business impact

Studio scales revenue without proportional operational cost. Client retention strengthens. Founder can pursue investment, partnerships, and new ventures.

### Success metric

**Exception rate** — &lt;15% of weekly founder OS time is exception handling; remainder is relationship, strategy, and growth.

## Era 6 — Founder Freedom

**Approximate horizon:** Years 8–10+

### Mission

The founder **leads the company** while KXD OS runs the studio — and the model becomes legible for other founder-led businesses.

### Primary focus

- Edition framework enables KXD Agency as Edition 1 — template for founder-led creative businesses
- Institutional knowledge is the product's deepest asset
- Founder time is predominantly external — clients, market, team culture, capital
- OS maturity is measured in calm, not capability count
- Optional: shared playbook library, standards marketplace (long-term, not required for Kreate)

### Major capabilities introduced

- Full lifecycle autonomy for standard client tiers
- Studio Knowledge exportable and teachable for acquisitions or partnerships
- Multi-role studio operation without founder in daily loop
- Quarterly business review generated from OS truth
- New client onboarding largely self-driving after founder sale

### Studio Intelligence maturity

**Level 4 mature** — with permanent trust boundaries

- OS runs; founder governs standards, relationships, and trust tier changes
- Learning loop has years of corrections, wins, and playbook outcomes
- Intelligence produces **less UI every year** — the mature OS is the quietest

### Founder experience

CEO mode. The OS is infrastructure — like electricity. Founder's KXD OS session is brief, strategic, and calm. Pride comes from what the studio delivers, not from managing software.

### Employee experience

Joins a studio with embedded institutional knowledge. Ramp time measured in days, not months. Quality bar is in the OS, not in the founder's head.

### Client experience

A decade of relationship memory. KXD knows their business. Growth conversations are rare and always evidence-backed. The portal feels like an extension of a long partnership.

### Business impact

Kreate operates at a scale and margin profile impossible without institutional OS. The system itself becomes a strategic asset — and potentially a product for other founder-led studios.

### Success metric

**Founder freedom index** — founder spends &gt;70% of professional time on relationships, strategy, leadership, investment, and growth; studio operations run without daily founder intervention.

## Permanent product pillars

Twelve pillars define the product map. **Studio Intelligence** is cross-cutting — it deepens every pillar rather than replacing any.

---

### 1. Studio

**Purpose:** The daily rhythm of the company — how the founder and team orient each day.

**Includes:** Today, launcher, operations home, notifications, command search.

**Relationship to Studio Intelligence:** Morning brief health, focus recommendations, operating inbox signals.

**Long-term vision:** One opening gesture tells you what kind of day it is and what needs you — nothing else.

---

### 2. Work

**Purpose:** Execution — what KXD is doing, for whom, by when.

**Includes:** Work Items portfolio, per-client boards, kanban, portal request spawning.

**Relationship to Studio Intelligence:** Today's Focus, next step recommendations, auto-spawn from events, standards check on completion.

**Long-term vision:** The founder asks "what should I work on next?" and the desk already has the answer.

---

### 3. Clients

**Purpose:** Portfolio view — who KXD serves and how the roster is performing.

**Includes:** Client portfolio, accounts intelligence, health at a glance.

**Relationship to Studio Intelligence:** Portfolio synthesis, attention routing, roster-level patterns.

**Long-term vision:** A living roster of partnerships — not a CRM table.

---

### 4. Client Command

**Purpose:** The home of each relationship — everything true about one client.

**Includes:** Overview, Timeline, Work, Actions, Intelligence, Financial, Communications, Proposals.

**Relationship to Studio Intelligence:** Primary context surface — memory, recommendations, prepared drafts, teaching.

**Long-term vision:** Walking into a client's wing — complete story, one room, tabbed depth.

---

### 5. Client Success

**Purpose:** Executive stewardship — care, counsel, and celebration.

**Includes:** Attention Needed, Recommendations, Growth Opportunities (rare), Wins.

**Relationship to Studio Intelligence:** Primary output channel for advisory patterns; evidence-required; sparse by design.

**Long-term vision:** The founder knows who needs a call — and rarely sees a false alarm.

---

### 6. Business Development

**Purpose:** Where new revenue forms — leads, proposals, conversion, launch.

**Includes:** Sales, proposals, conversion engine, Genesis, website audits, research leads.

**Relationship to Studio Intelligence:** Proposal Ready, pricing context, win pattern learning, funnel handoff orchestration.

**Long-term vision:** Pipeline with restraint — relationships forming, not rows being chased.

---

### 7. Creative

**Purpose:** The production floor — campaigns, assets, brand systems.

**Includes:** Creative Engine, flyers, video, social, brand kits, campaigns.

**Relationship to Studio Intelligence:** Standards check, brand voice from client canon, exemplar pull from prior work.

**Long-term vision:** Creative output matches KXD bar by default; founder reviews exception, not every asset.

---

### 8. Finance

**Purpose:** Revenue truth — retainers, invoices, billing health, financial command.

**Includes:** Financial Command, retainers, billing profiles, portfolio financial view.

**Relationship to Studio Intelligence:** Advises only (Trust Tier T3+) — never auto-commits money; surfaces risk and opportunity.

**Long-term vision:** Founder sees financial health in one calm view — no spreadsheet reconciliation.

---

### 9. Playbooks

**Purpose:** Repeatable studio rituals — the automation spine.

**Includes:** Playbook definitions, runs, steps, blockers, launch triggers.

**Relationship to Studio Intelligence:** Level 3–4 execution vehicle; Suggest Automation and Create Playbook feed this pillar.

**Long-term vision:** Every ritual the studio repeats more than three times lives in a playbook — founder approves once, OS runs forever.

---

### 10. Knowledge

**Purpose:** What KXD knows about how it operates — standards, SOPs, decisions, teaching canon.

**Includes:** Executive Notes, Strategy Vault, studio standards, correction-promoted rules, exemplar library.

**Relationship to Studio Intelligence:** Studio canon input; teaching source; standards check reference.

**Long-term vision:** Tribal knowledge retires — the OS is the senior employee who never leaves.

---

### 11. Portal

**Purpose:** The client-facing experience — how clients interact with KXD between meetings.

**Includes:** Requests, deliverables, reports, projects, invoices, website health, team.

**Relationship to Studio Intelligence:** Client-visible outputs are always human-committed; portal reflects OS truth in real time.

**Long-term vision:** Clients feel they have a private HQ — always current, never generic.

---

### 12. Founder

**Purpose:** The owner's chair — business health, strategic view, investment posture.

**Includes:** Founder dashboard, founder intelligence, platform progress, quarterly review.

**Relationship to Studio Intelligence:** Portfolio narrative, exception queue, autonomy governance, standards promotion.

**Long-term vision:** The founder governs the studio — does not operate it.

---

### Cross-cutting: Studio Intelligence

**Purpose:** The intelligence layer throughout KXD OS — not a pillar users navigate to.

**Relationship to all pillars:** Context, assistance patterns, learning, trust model, progressive autonomy.

**Long-term vision:** Invisible. The OS simply knows. More capability, less interface.

### Flywheel stages (plain language)

1. **Client work happens** — projects, requests, campaigns, calls, deliveries.
2. **Memory records it** — Activity Engine, timeline, communications, financial events.
3. **Work executes** — work items, playbooks, creative output, launches.
4. **Humans correct and approve** — founder edits, client feedback, won/lost outcomes.
5. **Knowledge accumulates** — client voice, studio standards, decision history, exemplars.
6. **Playbooks absorb patterns** — repeatable sequences become approved automation.
7. **Studio Intelligence improves** — better recommendations, better preparation, fewer false signals.
8. **Decisions improve** — faster, more consistent, evidence-backed.
9. **Time is reclaimed** — founder and team capacity returns.
10. **More client work with higher quality** — outcomes improve, memory deepens, flywheel accelerates.

### Flywheel rules

- **Every loop must close** — work without memory is wasted; memory without learning is archive
- **Corrections are fuel** — the most valuable flywheel input is founder edits
- **Promotion requires human** — patterns become playbooks only with approval
- **Client outcomes are the scoreboard** — not features shipped or insights generated
- **Silence is healthy** — a quiet dashboard with good outcomes means the flywheel is working

## The product moat

KXD OS becomes increasingly difficult to compete against — not because of features, but because of **accumulated studio truth**.

### Why a new tool cannot catch up quickly

| Moat layer | What accumulates | Why it compounds |
|------------|------------------|------------------|
| **Relationship history** | Years of timeline events per client | Cannot be imported from HubSpot as narrative truth |
| **Decision record** | Why KXD chose X, what was rejected, what was corrected | Judgment fossilized — not data, wisdom |
| **Client voice** | Edit history, approved copy, communication tone | Generic AI cannot replicate without years of corrections |
| **Operating standards** | KXD-specific quality bar in Knowledge vault | Competitors have tools; Kreate has a canon |
| **Playbook library** | Approved rituals tuned to how Kreate actually works | Automation encodes institutional habit |
| **Learning from outcomes** | Which recommendations led to wins vs noise | Suppression and promotion tuned over years |
| **Trust calibration** | What the founder approves vs dismisses | Personal operating rhythm embedded in the OS |
| **Team teaching** | In-context SOPs from real exemplars | New hires ramp on Kreate's memory, not generic docs |
| **Cross-pillar orchestration** | BD → Genesis → Command → Work → Portal as one story | Integration depth, not API checkboxes |

### What the moat is not

- Not a feature checklist — Monday can add kanban
- Not a model — ChatGPT can draft email
- Not a brand — dark UI can be copied
- Not data volume — CRMs hoard records without memory

### What the moat is

**Institutional operating knowledge** — the accumulated judgment of how Kreate by Design runs, encoded in relationship memory, standards, playbooks, corrections, and outcomes — experienced as calm competence, not as software complexity.

A competitor starting today has software. Kreate, ten years in, has **a studio that remembers**.

### Moat depth over time

| Year | Moat depth |
|------|------------|
| 1 | Better than spreadsheets — unified environment |
| 3 | Client relationships are fully storied in OS |
| 5 | Playbooks run half the studio; corrections tuned intelligence |
| 7 | New employee productive in days; clients feel decade-long memory |
| 10 | OS is strategic asset — replicating Kreate means replicating years of encoded judgment |

## Ten years from now — a narrative

*If KXD OS becomes the operating system for founder-led businesses, this is what people will say.*

---

**The founder**

"I don't run the studio on software. The studio runs — and I lead."

Ten years in, the founder doesn't describe KXD OS as a tool they use. They describe it the way a pilot describes an aircraft they've flown for a decade — intimately known, trusted, invisible during normal flight.

They open it in the morning the way someone opens a briefcase that already has today's papers sorted. Not twenty dashboards. Not a chat window asking how it can help. A brief: healthy, three things if anything needs them, otherwise clear.

They take client calls without prep anxiety. The relationship is there — what was promised, what was delivered, what was said last quarter, what the client's voice sounds like in writing. They edit two sentences on a prepared follow-up and send it. The timeline records the call without being asked.

They don't remember the last time they manually created a monthly deliverable reminder. They do remember when a genuine growth conversation happened — because it was rare, evidence-backed, and right. Most clients had zero growth signals showing. That was correct.

They spend most of their week with people — clients, partners, team, investors. The OS handles the rhythm of delivery. When something needs them, it arrives as an exception — blocked, overdue, client waiting, trust-tier decision — not as noise.

If you ask what KXD OS is, they pause. They don't say "CRM" or "project manager." They say: *"It's how we run."*

---

**The employee**

"I joined and the studio already knew how to work."

New employees don't inherit a folder of PDFs and a patient founder. They inherit a system that teaches in context — show me how, once, on the thing you're actually doing. Standards checks catch voice and quality before embarrassment. The timeline tells them the client story without a handoff meeting.

They feel the bar is high but reachable — because the bar is in the work, not in the founder's mood that day.

Senior people don't hoard context. Junior people don't stall on ambiguity. The OS holds the middle.

They don't think about "AI features." They think: the proposal was already drafted, the reply was ready, the next step was obvious. That's just how KXD works.

---

**The client**

"They never drop the thread."

Clients don't experience KXD OS directly — they experience its effects. Follow-ups reference last quarter's conversation. Deliverables arrive on rhythm. The portal is current without being asked. When KXD suggests something, it's because something real changed — not because it's quarter-end.

Long-term clients say KXD knows their business. New clients say KXD feels unusually organized for a creative studio. Both are right.

The portal doesn't feel like a ticket system. It feels like a private room at a firm that has been paying attention.

---

**The industry observer**

"They built the thing everyone else bolted on."

Ten years from now, the market is full of AI assistants, CRMs with copilots, and agency tools with dashboards. Most founders are busier than before — more software, same chaos.

KXD OS will be discussed differently — not as the most features, but as the most **calm**. The studio that got quieter as it got smarter. The founder who actually got time back.

People who understand operations will recognize the moat: not the code, but the memory. Years of relationship truth, encoded standards, and approved playbooks that cannot be installed from a marketplace.

Whether KXD OS ever ships as a product for other founder-led studios, the architecture of the idea will be clear: **the operating system is the institutional memory of the company, and the company gets better every time it works.**

## How to use this document

### For product decisions

- Which **era** does this feature advance?
- Does it strengthen the **flywheel** or spin without closing the loop?
- Does it deepen the **moat** or add replaceable surface area?
- Does it pass the **Constitution** — Respect Time, calm, one executive question?

### For prioritization

Near-term product priority generally follows era order — but pillars mature unevenly. Client Command Memory may advance while Creative Guidance lags. That is acceptable. The roadmap is directional, not sequential lockstep.

### For Cursor agents

When scoping work, cite:

> `docs/KXD-OS-PRODUCT-ROADMAP.md` — [Era name] · [Pillar] · flywheel stage · Constitution + Studio Intelligence Architecture

### Living document

Update when:

- An era's success metric is achieved and the next era's focus sharpens
- A pillar's long-term vision materially changes
- Edition strategy for founder-led businesses advances
- Studio Intelligence maturity shifts platform-wide

Do **not** update for individual engineering phase completion — that belongs in the platform registry.
